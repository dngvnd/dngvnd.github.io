About Me

My name is Duc Duy NGUYEN, you can call by my English name "Joey".

Currently, I'm a PhD candidate at Norwegian University of Science and Technology (NTNU) specializing in clean energy and combustion technology. With experience in hydrogen fuel research, engine development, and facilities engineering, I focus on developing sustainable solutions for marine applications. My research centers on ammonia and hydrogen as clean-burning fuels for marine engines, combining theoretical modeling with practical experimentation.

I had 5 years of experience in the field of thermal engineering, and 5 years of experience in the field of internal combustion engine and is a specialist in the field of spark-ignition combustion, pressure charging, hydrogen fuel.

I originally had a career in industry, spending 5 years working as project engineer. Before moving to NTNU, I have served as a graduate researcher at the Clean Combustion Research Center (Now Clean Energy Research Platform) at KAUST in Saudi Arabia, and Korea Institute of Machinery and Materials in Korea. I hold a Master's degree in Mechanical Engineering from University of Science and Technology in Korea.
